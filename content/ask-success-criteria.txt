<h1 style="text-align: center; font-size: 20pt; font-weight: bold;">Success Criteria Definition</h1>
<h1 style="text-align: center; font-size: 16pt; font-weight: bold;">Define specific, measurable criteria to evaluate solution effectiveness</h1>

<h2>SMART Success Criteria</h2>

<h3>Criterion 1: Reduced employee turnover rate</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Specific:</strong> The overall employee turnover rate for either one or multiple transportation companies</li>
<li><strong>Measurable:</strong> Turnover rate over a set period of time</li>
<li><strong>Achievable:</strong> YES</li>
<li><strong>Relevant:</strong> Yes it addresses the core problem</li>
<li><strong>Time-bound:</strong> Annually</li>
</ul>

<h3>Criterion 2: Increased employee satisfaction</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Specific:</strong> Employee satisfaction survey results</li>
<li><strong>Measurable:</strong> Through higher scores on employee satisfaction surveys or interviews</li>
<li><strong>Achievable:</strong> YES</li>
<li><strong>Relevant:</strong> YES</li>
<li><strong>Time-bound:</strong> QUARTERLY OR ANNUALLY</li>
</ul>

<h2>Measurement Framework</h2>

<h3>Quantitative Metrics</h3>
<ul>
<li>Turnover rate</li>
<li>Overall productivity</li>
<li>Employee satisfaction</li>
</ul>

<h3>Qualitative Indicators</h3>
<ul>
<li>Employee perceptions of distributive/procedural justice</li>
<li>General attitudes towards work environment</li>
</ul>

<h2>Leading vs. Lagging Indicators</h2>

<h4>Leading Indicators (Early signals of progress)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Increased employee satisfaction</li>
</ul>

<h4>Lagging Indicators (Final outcomes)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Reduced overall turnover rate</li>
<li>Reduced turnover intention</li>
</ul>



<h2>Stakeholder-Specific Success Criteria</h2>

<h3>Management Success Measures</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduced turnover rate</li>
<li>Decreased costs associated with staff shortages</li>
</ul>

<h3>Employee Success Measures</h3>
<ul>
<li>Increased overall job satisfaction associated with intrinsic motivation</li>
<li>Increased perceptions of organizational fairness</li>
<li>Implementation of wellness programs and policies</li>
</ul>

<h3>Customer Success Measures</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduction in delayed deliveries.</li>
<li>Decrease in product shortages for retailers and receiving businesses.</li>
</ul>


<h2>Timeline for Assessment</h2>

<h3>Short-term (30-90 days)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Updated scheduling frameworks</li>
</ul>

<h3>Medium-term (3-6 months)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Implementation of employee health & wellness policies & programs</li>
<li>Adjustment to wages/salaries</li>
<li>Implementation of bonuses and/or other benefits</li>
<li>Improved overall management quality</li>
</ul>

<h3>Long-term (6-12 months)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Shift in organizational culture</li>
<li>Job redesign around proposed interventions</li>
<li>Overhauled procedural/distributive justice practices</li>
</ul>


---
INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Be specific and measurable - avoid vague terms like "better" or "improved"
2. Ensure criteria are achievable given your resources and timeline
3. Include both quantitative and qualitative measures
4. Consider different stakeholder perspectives on success
5. Plan how you'll actually collect the measurement data
