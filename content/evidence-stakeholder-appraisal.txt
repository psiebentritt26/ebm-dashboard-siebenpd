# Stakeholder Evidence: Quality Assessment
# Evaluate the credibility and usefulness of stakeholder evidence collected

## Overview
**Total Stakeholder Groups Assessed:** 3
**Evidence Sources:** Published surveys, semi-structured interviews, practitioner articles
**Assessment Date:** November 21, 2025

---

## Representativeness

### Stakeholder Group 1: Long-Haul Truck Drivers (Employees)

**Sample Characteristics:**
- **Sample Size:** 33 respondents
- **Data Source:** Aryal et al. (2023) semi-structured interviews

**Representativeness Assessment:**
- **Strengths:** Represents long-haul truck drivers
- **Limitations:** Smaller sample size, possibly not randomly selected
- **Biases:** Selection bias, potential social desirability bias
- **Generalizability:** Yes, but only if sample was randomly selected from different companies or geographic areas

---

### Stakeholder Group 2: Transportation Company Managers/Supply chain managers

**Sample Characteristics:**
- **Sample Size:** Single practitioner article (Glodziak, 2022)
- **Data Sources:** Practitioner perspective from CEO of supply chain company, Worker Well-Being paper
- **Expertise Level:** CEO/executive level perspective/managerial perspectives

**Representativeness Assessment:**
- **Strengths:** Executive supply chain industry knowledge, and company managerial insight
- **Limitations:** Two sources, may not represent views of other companies
- **Biases:** Selection bias, only two sources available
- **Generalizability:** Only generalizable if additional research is found

---

### Stakeholder Group 3: Partnered Shipping Companies (Customers)

**Sample Characteristics:**
- **Sample Size:** Unclear from survey
- **Data Source:** Inbound Logistics 2025 shipper survey

**Representativeness Assessment:**
- **Strengths:** Survey format captures broader perspectives
- **Limitations:** Unclear sample size and selection methods, unclear which shipping companies responded
- **Biases:** Respondants may be biased towards Inbound Logistics company
- **Generalizability:** Not generalizable until critical limitations above are resolved

---

### Overall Representativeness Rating

**High Representativeness (✅):**
- Drivers/employees
- Managers

**Moderate Representativeness (⚠️):**
- Supply chain executives

**Low Representativeness (❌):**
- Partnered shipping companies

## Impact Assessment

### Evidence Quality by Stakeholder Group

#### Drivers (Employees)
- **Power (High/med/low):** Low
- **Effect:** Benefits from interventions if implemented properly

#### Managers/Supply Chain Executives
- **Power:** Medium-high
- **Effect:** Benefits from reduced turnover from properly implemented interventions, but may incur costs of new programs and regulations

#### Customers (Shipping Companies)
- **Power:** Medium
- **Effect:** Benefits from reduced driver turnover and greater shipment timeliness, but may pay more if costs rise from interventions

---

## Confidence

**Overall confidence in stakeholder evidence:** Medium

**Strengths:**
- Good baseline insights
- Surveys capture broad perspectives
- Insights from executives as well as managers

**Weaknesses:**
- Little information on origins of survey respondants
- Limited sample sizes
- Findings less generalizable until weaknesses are corrected
